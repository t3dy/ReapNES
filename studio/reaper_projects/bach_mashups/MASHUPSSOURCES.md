# Classical x NES Mashups — Sources and Design Rationale

This directory contains 681 REAPER projects that pair classical MIDI
performances with NES synthesizer timbres ripped from game ROMs. Each
project uses the ReapNES APU JSFX plugin to render the classical music
through authentic Nintendo pulse and triangle wave synthesis.

Generated by `scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py`.

---

## How It Works

### The NES Sound Architecture

The Nintendo Entertainment System has four sound channels:

| Channel | Waveform | Duty Cycles | Role in Mashups |
|---------|----------|-------------|-----------------|
| **Pulse 1** | Square wave | 12.5%, 25%, 50%, 75% | Lead melody / soprano voice |
| **Pulse 2** | Square wave | 12.5%, 25%, 50%, 75% | Countermelody / alto voice |
| **Triangle** | Triangle wave | Fixed (no duty control) | Bass line / lowest voice |
| **Noise** | Pseudorandom | Tonal or white | Percussion (if present) |

Each NES game's sound engine chooses specific **duty cycle** settings for
its pulse channels, giving every game a distinctive timbre. A 50% duty
cycle produces a warm, full square wave. A 12.5% duty cycle produces a
thin, buzzy, metallic tone. These timbral signatures are what make
Castlevania sound different from Contra, even though both use the same
hardware.

### Voice Assignment Logic

When a classical MIDI file is loaded, the auto-mapper assigns voices
to NES channels using these rules:

1. **Lowest average pitch** among melodic tracks becomes the **triangle**
   (bass), since the NES triangle channel naturally sits an octave below
   the pulse channels for the same timer period (32-step vs 16-step
   hardware sequencer).

2. **Busiest melodic track** (most notes) becomes **Pulse 1** (lead).
   In Bach fugues this is typically the subject-carrying voice; in
   Beethoven sonatas it is the right-hand melody.

3. **Second busiest melodic track** becomes **Pulse 2** (countermelody).

4. **MIDI channel 9** (GM drums) maps to **Noise**, though most of
   these classical pieces have no percussion.

5. **Single-voice pieces** go to Pulse 1 rather than triangle, because
   the pulse wave is more musically expressive with duty cycle control.

This means a 3-voice Bach fugue maps perfectly to Pulse 1 + Pulse 2 +
Triangle — three independent contrapuntal lines through three independent
NES channels.

### Mood Compatibility Scoring

Every combination is scored 1–10 based on the emotional character of the
classical piece matched against the emotional character of the game stage.
The score uses a symmetric lookup table:

- **9/10**: Strong match (dark piece + dark stage, heroic + heroic)
- **7-8/10**: Good complementary pairing (heroic + intense, dark + tense)
- **5-6/10**: Neutral or interesting contrast
- **3-4/10**: Deliberate clash (bright piece + dark stage)

All combinations are generated regardless of score. The score helps
identify which pairings to listen to first.

## Classical Sources

### J.S. Bach (44 pieces, 513 projects)

Bach is the core of this collection because his contrapuntal writing
maps structurally to the NES hardware. A 3-voice fugue has exactly
three independent melodic lines — one per NES melodic channel.

| Category | Pieces | Voices | Why They Work |
|----------|--------|--------|---------------|
| **Two-Part Inventions** (14) | Nos. 1–5, 7–15 | 2 | Pure two-voice counterpoint. Pulse 1 + Pulse 2 dueling with no bass needed. Ideal for hearing duty cycle contrast. |
| **Three-Part Sinfonias** (1) | No. 1 in C | 3 | Three independent voices = perfect NES channel mapping. |
| **WTC Book 1 Fugues** (9) | Nos. 1–5, 7, 9, 10, 14 | 2–4 | The fugues are the jewels. 3-voice fugues (1, 2, 3, 7, 9) use all three melodic channels. 4-voice fugues (4, 5, 14) add the noise channel as a rhythmic anchor. |
| **WTC Book 1 Preludes** (7) | Nos. 1, 2, 4, 6, 9, 10, 12 | 1–4 | Range from single arpeggiated lines (Prelude 1) to full 4-voice textures. |
| **Goldberg Variations** (5) | Aria, Vars 1, 3, 4, 5, 6 | 2–4 | The canons (Var 3, Var 6) are especially effective — strict imitation between pulse channels. |
| **Cello Suites** (3) | Suite 1: Prelude, Courante, Gigue | 2–4 | Originally solo cello; the implied polyphony maps surprisingly well to multi-channel NES. |
| **Organ works** (2) | Toccata & Fugue BWV 565, Prelude & Fugue BWV 543 | 2–3 | Dramatic organ textures through buzzy pulse waves. |
| **BWV Collection** (2) | BWV 846, 847 | 3–4 | Complete Prelude + Fugue pairs in single files. |

**MIDI sources**: Kunst der Fuge (kunstderfuge.com), CC-licensed MIDI
performances downloaded to `~/Downloads/`. BWV 846–850 from a separate
ZIP collection extracted to `studio/midi/classical/bach_collection/`.

## NES Game Timbres

Each game stage contributes a specific duty cycle configuration extracted
from the original ROM data via CC12 (duty cycle) automation in the ripped
MIDI files. The duty cycles were identified by analyzing the dominant CC12
values per channel across each stage's music.

### Castlevania 1 (Konami, 1987) — 5 stages

The Castlevania soundtrack by Kinuyo Yamashita uses varied duty cycles
to give each stage a distinct emotional color.

| Stage | P1 Duty | P2 Duty | Mood | Timbral Character |
|-------|---------|---------|------|-------------------|
| **Vampire Killer** | 50% | 25% | Heroic | Classic balanced NES sound — warm lead with bright harmony. The signature CV1 sound. |
| **Stalker** | 50% | 50% | Atmospheric | Full square waves on both pulses, dense and enveloping. |
| **Wicked Child** | 25% | 50% | Bright | Bright punchy lead with warm square harmony. Inverted from Vampire Killer. |
| **Heart of Fire** | 25% | 25% | Intense | Both pulses at 25% — bright, cutting, aggressive. |
| **Nothing to Lose** | 50% | 12.5% | Dark | Warm square lead against thin buzzy pad — tension and unease. |

### Contra (Konami, 1988) — 4 stages

Contra's sound engine (same Konami driver family as CV1) uses more
extreme duty cycle choices for its military sci-fi atmosphere.

| Stage | P1 Duty | P2 Duty | Mood | Timbral Character |
|-------|---------|---------|------|-------------------|
| **Jungle** | 75% | 25% | Aggressive | Wide asymmetric pulses — punchy, military feel. |
| **Waterfall** | 12.5% | 12.5% | Eerie | Both pulses at minimum duty — thin, metallic, haunting. |
| **Flame Fortress** | 12.5% | 12.5% | Tense | Same thin pulses as Waterfall but claustrophobic, urgent. |
| **Maze Fortress** | 75% | 12.5% | Ominous | Maximum contrast — wide warm lead vs razor-thin staccato. |

### Journey to Silius (Sunsoft, 1990) — 5 stages

Journey to Silius is famous for its aggressive, bass-heavy soundtrack
by Naoki Kodaka. Sunsoft's sound engine pushed the NES hardware harder
than most, with rapid duty cycle switching and deep bass lines.

| Stage | P1 Duty | P2 Duty | Mood | Timbral Character |
|-------|---------|---------|------|-------------------|
| **Stage 1 & 5** | 25% | 25% | Heroic | Dual bright 25% pulses — classic Sunsoft driving energy. |
| **Stage 2** | 12.5% | 12.5% | Eerie | Both thin — the haunting, metallic Sunsoft signature. |
| **Stage 3** | 25% | 50% | Intense | Bright lead against warm harmony — driving action. |
| **Stage 4** | 25% | 50% | Dark | Same duty split as Stage 3 but in a darker musical context. |
| **Ending Theme** | 75% | 25% | Heroic | Wide triumphant lead with bright counterpoint — resolution. |

### Gradius (Konami, 1986) — 3 stages

Gradius uses the early Konami sound engine. Fewer stages were extracted

### Ghosts 'n' Goblins (Capcom/Micronics, 1986) — 1 stage

## Why These Pairings Work

### Duty Cycle as Orchestration

In a traditional orchestra, a composer chooses instruments for their
timbre: warm cellos for a melody, bright trumpets for a fanfare, muted
strings for tension. On the NES, the equivalent choice is the **duty
cycle**. Each game's sound designer made these choices for dramatic
reasons, and those same reasons apply when we route classical music
through the same timbres:

- **50% duty (warm square)**: Equivalent to a clarinet or warm string
  section. Works for lyrical melodies — Fur Elise, Pathetique, Goldberg
  Aria. Best stages: CV1 Vampire Killer, CV1 Stalker, BC Enemy Base.

- **25% duty (bright pulse)**: Equivalent to an oboe or bright brass.
  Works for energetic, articulate passages — Rondo alla Turca, WTC
  fugue subjects, Symphony No.5 fate motif. Best stages: CV1 Wicked
  Child, JtS Stage 1, BC Munitions Base.

- **12.5% duty (thin buzz)**: Equivalent to a muted trumpet or
  harpsichord. Works for eerie, tense, or archaic textures — Moonlight
  Mv1, dark fugues, Symphony 40. Best stages: Contra Waterfall, JtS
  Stage 2, BC Neutral Zone, Gradius Boss.

- **75% duty (wide asymmetric)**: Equivalent to a trombone or organ
  reed stop. Bold and aggressive. Works for dramatic, powerful music —
  Toccata & Fugue, Symphony 5, heroic fugues. Best stages: Contra
  Jungle, Gradius Mountains, BC Albatross Tower.

### Three Voices = Three Channels

The NES has exactly three melodic channels. Bach's three-voice fugues
and sinfonias, Mozart's three-part textures, and Beethoven's
melody+accompaniment+bass piano writing all naturally decompose into
three independent lines. This is why we prioritized 3-voice pieces —
every voice gets its own dedicated NES channel with no doubling, dropout,
or compromise.

Four-voice pieces (WTC Fugue 4, Symphony No.5) still work because the
auto-mapper assigns the fourth voice to the noise channel or merges it
into the busiest pulse, but the 3-voice pieces produce the cleanest
results.

### The Triangle as Bass

The NES triangle channel produces a pitch one octave below the pulse
channels for the same timer period (32-step vs 16-step sequencer — a
hardware fact). This means the lowest voice in any classical piece
naturally sits in the right register when assigned to triangle. Bach's
bass lines, Beethoven's left-hand octaves, and Mozart's Alberti bass
patterns all land correctly without transposition.

## How to Regenerate

```bash
# All projects (all composers, all games)
python scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py --generate-all

# Filter by composer
python scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py --generate-all --composer Mozart
python scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py --generate-all --composer Beethoven

# Filter by game
python scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py --generate-all --game BionicCommando

# Filter by voice count (3-voice pieces only)
python scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py --generate-all --voices 3

# Combine filters
python scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py --generate-all --game Contra --composer Beethoven

# List all combinations with mood scores
python scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py --list --voices 3 --new-games

# Render top-scoring combinations to WAV
python scripts/bach_nes_mashup.py --render-best --top 20
```
