Castlevania (NES, 1986) — Complete Soundtrack
Extracted directly from the ROM using NES Music Studio

Every track in this video was parsed from the original Castlevania ROM data — no audio recording, no emulator capture. The Konami pre-VRC sound driver (Maezawa variant) was reverse-engineered and each channel's note data, volume envelopes, and timing were decoded from the raw bytes.

The audio is synthesized from a frame-accurate intermediate representation that models the NES APU's pulse wave duty cycles, triangle wave, and noise channel. Volume envelopes use the two-phase parametric model decoded from the driver: fast attack decay controlled by fade_start, followed by a release phase controlled by fade_step. Triangle channel articulation is modeled via the APU linear counter.

Technical Details:

- Source: Castlevania (U) (V1.0) NES ROM
- Driver: Konami Pre-VRC (Maezawa variant, ~1986)
- Extraction: Static ROM parsing (Pipeline A) with frame-accurate IR
- Validation: Zero pitch mismatches against Mesen APU trace on Vampire Killer
- Synthesis: Python NES APU emulation (pulse duty cycles, triangle 32-step, noise LFSR)
- Tools: NES Music Studio (github.com/t3dy/NESMusicStudio)
